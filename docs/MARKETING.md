# Perigee — Positioning brief

The single source of truth for how Perigee is named and sold. Everything downstream (README H1,
landing copy, repo description, launch kit) keys off this.

## Audience & positioning

**Who it's for.** Daily-puzzle players who like reading a trajectory — the Angry Birds arc, the
Kerbal Space Program orbit — but don't want a physics sandbox with no goal or a campaign that
eats an evening. Someone who does the Wordle-slot puzzle at lunch and would happily add one that
scratches a spatial, spaceflight itch instead of a verbal one.

**The one pain.** Physics puzzle games mostly cheat: the "trajectory" is a canned animation, or
it's a real simulation buried in a sandbox with nothing to solve. Neither gives you the specific
satisfaction of reading a gravity well correctly and watching one committed shot play out.

**The core benefit.** Aim once, and real n-body gravity flies the rest. One shared level a day,
a par to beat, a five-minute sit-down. The whole skill is reading the physics, not piloting.

## Name

**Perigee** — kept as the final display name. It clears the rubric:

- Perigee is the point in an orbit closest to the body it circles. The entire game is threading
  tight, close gravity-assist passes, so the name *is* the mechanic, not decoration.
- One real word, three syllables, easy to say and spell; no `-ly` / `-ify` / `AI-` slop suffix.
- Ownable: an astronomy term, not a taken product brand, and it sits naturally in the
  mission-control register the game already wears.

## Tagline

**Aim once. Real gravity does the rest.**

Benefit first, states the one input and the payoff, names no feature it doesn't have.

## Voice

First person, plain, concrete. Charlie talking about a thing he made, not a launch announcement.
No em-dashes in the marketing copy, no buzzword set (leverage / seamless / unlock / robust / …),
every claim backed by a specific capability of this build.
